## Overview

<Callout type="info">
This content is educational and describes how the Margin Invest engine model works. Suggested allocations are model outputs, not personal investment recommendations. Always consider your own financial situation before making investment decisions.
</Callout>

Margin Invest doesn't just identify investment candidates — it suggests how much to allocate based on conviction strength and opportunity type. Position sizing is one of the most overlooked aspects of portfolio construction, and the engine treats it as a first-class output rather than an afterthought.

## Maximum Positions

The model portfolio holds a maximum of **10 positions**. This constraint ensures meaningful concentration in high-conviction ideas while maintaining diversification. With 10 slots, each position carries enough weight to impact overall returns — unlike a 50-stock portfolio where individual picks barely move the needle.

Not all 10 slots need to be filled. The engine prioritizes quality over quantity. If only 4 candidates pass all conviction gates, the model will suggest a 4-position portfolio rather than diluting conviction by lowering the bar to fill all 10 slots.

## Sizing by Opportunity Type

The engine assigns a suggested allocation percentage based on two dimensions: the **opportunity type** (Compounder, Mispricing, or Both) and the **conviction level** (EXCEPTIONAL, HIGH, or WATCHLIST).

| Opportunity Type | EXCEPTIONAL | HIGH | WATCHLIST |
|-----------------|-------------|------|-----------|
| Compounder      | 15%         | 8%   | 0%        |
| Mispricing      | 12%         | 6%   | 0%        |
| Both            | 20%         | 10%  | 0%        |

<Callout type="tip">
WATCHLIST candidates always receive 0% allocation. This is by design — if a stock hasn't passed all conviction gates, the engine won't suggest you take a position. WATCHLIST means "monitor this candidate for changes," not "take a small position."
</Callout>

## Why "Both" Gets the Largest Allocation

Stocks qualifying on both Track A (Compounder) and Track B (Mispricing) represent the strongest signal the engine can produce. They combine two independent sources of conviction:

- **Compounder qualities**: High-quality operations, durable competitive advantages, and consistent execution
- **Mispricing qualities**: The market is temporarily undervaluing the stock relative to its fundamentals

This combination — a great business at a dislocated price — is rare. The 20% EXCEPTIONAL allocation for "Both" reflects this rarity and the compounding of two independent conviction signals, compared to 15% for Compounder-only or 12% for Mispricing-only.

## Understanding the Numbers

<Example title="Portfolio Example">
Consider a model portfolio with 5 qualifying candidates:

- 3 HIGH Compounders at 8% each = **24%**
- 1 EXCEPTIONAL Mispricing at 12% = **12%**
- 1 HIGH Both at 10% = **10%**
- **Total allocated: 46%**
- Remaining 54%: cash or existing positions not managed by the model

This is a model allocation, not a prescription. The engine is suggesting that roughly half of a hypothetical portfolio could be deployed across these 5 candidates, with the remaining capital held in reserve or allocated to positions outside the model's scope.
</Example>

## Risk Management Principles

### Sector diversification

Even if multiple candidates in the same sector qualify, consider your total sector exposure. The engine scores each stock independently — it doesn't automatically balance across sectors. That responsibility falls to you as the portfolio manager.

### Conviction-proportional sizing

Higher conviction equals a larger position. This is intentional — the engine is expressing that it has stronger evidence for some candidates than others. A 15% EXCEPTIONAL Compounder suggestion carries meaningfully more model confidence than an 8% HIGH Compounder suggestion.

### Cash as a position

Not being fully invested is a valid output. If the engine only finds 3-4 qualifying candidates, it's telling you the opportunity set is limited. Forcing capital into marginal ideas to achieve full investment is one of the most common portfolio construction mistakes.

## Common Pitfalls

<Callout type="warning">
**Over-concentrating in one sector**: Three Compounder candidates from the Tech sector might all look strong individually, but a 24% allocation to one sector significantly increases your portfolio's risk to sector-specific events. Consider your total sector exposure before following each suggestion independently.
</Callout>

<Callout type="warning">
**Treating suggested sizes as exact targets**: The percentages are model outputs based on conviction strength. Your actual position sizes should account for your risk tolerance, portfolio size, existing holdings, and personal circumstances. Use them as a starting framework, not as precise instructions.
</Callout>

<Callout type="warning">
**Ignoring position sizing entirely**: Some users only look at conviction levels and skip the allocation suggestions. The sizing contains meaningful information — a 15% suggestion vs an 8% suggestion reflects meaningfully different conviction strength from the engine's factor analysis.
</Callout>

<Callout type="warning">
**Scaling up WATCHLIST positions**: The engine allocates 0% to WATCHLIST for a reason. Don't override this by manually taking positions in WATCHLIST candidates. If a stock is on the WATCHLIST, the engine is saying it hasn't cleared enough conviction gates to warrant capital allocation yet.
</Callout>
