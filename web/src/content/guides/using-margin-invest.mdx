## Getting Started

<Callout type="info">
This guide is educational content explaining how to use the platform effectively. It is not investment advice. Always do your own research before making investment decisions.
</Callout>

Margin Invest is a deterministic scoring platform -- it does the analytical work, you make the decisions. Rather than offering opinions or predictions, the engine surfaces high-conviction candidates using a dual-track approach that evaluates every stock as both a potential Compounder and a potential Mispricing opportunity.

The value you get from the platform depends entirely on how you integrate its signals into your own research process. This guide walks through practical workflows, reading the dashboard, interpreting results, and avoiding common mistakes.

## Typical Workflow

### Weekly Review (Recommended)

A weekly check is the sweet spot for most users. Scores update as new data arrives, but meaningful changes to conviction levels typically happen on a weekly or quarterly cadence.

1. **Check the dashboard** for new candidates or conviction level changes since your last visit
2. **Review any newly eliminated stocks** -- understanding why a previously scored stock was removed helps calibrate your expectations
3. **Examine HIGH and EXCEPTIONAL conviction candidates** in detail by expanding their cards
4. **Use the factor breakdown** to understand what is driving each score, not just the final conviction level
5. **Cross-reference with your own research** before making any decisions -- the platform is one input, not the only input

### Daily Check (Optional)

A quick daily scan works well if you want to stay on top of fast-moving changes:

- Scan for conviction level changes on existing candidates
- Note any new EXCEPTIONAL candidates -- these are rare and worth immediate attention when they appear

## Reading the Dashboard

Each candidate card on the dashboard displays the key information you need at a glance:

- **Ticker and company name** for quick identification
- **Conviction badge** showing the current conviction level (EXCEPTIONAL, HIGH, WATCHLIST, or NONE)
- **Opportunity type** indicating whether the stock qualified as a Compounder, Mispricing, or Both
- **Sector color bar** providing a visual indicator of which GICS sector the company belongs to

Cards are ordered by conviction strength, so the strongest candidates always appear first.

<Callout type="tip">
The sector color bar on each card helps you quickly spot concentration in a single sector. If you see a wall of the same color at the top of your dashboard, you may want to think carefully about diversification before acting.
</Callout>

## Interpreting Conviction Levels

Each conviction level carries a specific meaning and suggests a different course of action:

| Level | Meaning | Action |
|-------|---------|--------|
| EXCEPTIONAL | Extremely rare, top-tier on all metrics | Deep research priority |
| HIGH | Strong across all gates, actionable signal | Evaluate for portfolio inclusion |
| WATCHLIST | Shows promise, missing 1+ gate | Monitor, do not act |
| NONE | Below threshold | No action needed |

<Callout type="warning">
WATCHLIST is NOT a buy signal. It means the stock is interesting but not yet qualified. Acting on WATCHLIST candidates defeats the purpose of the conviction system.
</Callout>

## Expanding a Candidate

When you click on a card, the detail panel shows the full picture behind the conviction level:

- **Factor breakdown**: Which gates passed and which failed on each track, with individual factor scores
- **Score details**: The raw multiplicative scores that produced the final conviction level
- **Position sizing**: Suggested allocation percentage based on conviction strength and opportunity type
- **Filter results**: Whether all elimination filters passed, and which specific filters failed if any did not

The factor breakdown is the most important section for understanding a candidate. It tells you not just the final score, but the story behind it -- which factors are contributing strength and which are dragging the score down.

## Opportunity Types

The dual-track engine can qualify a stock on one or both tracks, producing three possible opportunity types:

- **Compounder**: Qualified on Track A -- a company that compounds wealth through quality operations, strong returns on capital, and consistent execution. Compounders typically warrant a longer holding period because the thesis is about sustained quality, not a single catalyst.
- **Mispricing**: Qualified on Track B -- a stock trading well below intrinsic value with identifiable catalysts for revaluation. Mispricing opportunities may have a shorter holding period because the thesis resolves when the market reprices the stock.
- **Both**: Qualified on both tracks -- the strongest possible signal. A high-quality compounder that is also meaningfully undervalued. These candidates receive the largest suggested allocation because both tracks independently confirm the opportunity.

## Decision Checklist

Before acting on any candidate, work through these questions:

1. Is the conviction level HIGH or EXCEPTIONAL? (Never act on WATCHLIST)
2. Do I understand which track or tracks qualified and why?
3. Have I reviewed the factor breakdown for any yellow flags?
4. Does this fit my portfolio's sector allocation?
5. Am I comfortable with the suggested position size, or do I want to adjust?
6. Have I done my own independent research beyond the platform's analysis?

<Callout type="tip">
This checklist is a starting point. Develop your own process that incorporates Margin Invest's signals alongside your existing research workflow.
</Callout>

## Common Pitfalls

<Callout type="warning">
**Acting on WATCHLIST candidates**: The temptation to "get in early" on a WATCHLIST stock is strong. Resist it. WATCHLIST means one or more quality gates have not been met. Wait for the score to improve or move on.
</Callout>

<Callout type="warning">
**Ignoring opportunity type context**: A Compounder and a Mispricing are fundamentally different opportunities. A Compounder is about sustained quality; a Mispricing is about temporary dislocation. Your holding period, position size, and exit criteria should differ accordingly.
</Callout>

<Callout type="warning">
**Treating scores as static**: Scores update as new data arrives -- quarterly financials, price changes, insider activity. A HIGH conviction today could become WATCHLIST next quarter if fundamentals deteriorate. Check back regularly.
</Callout>

<Callout type="warning">
**Over-concentrating in one sector**: Even if several candidates in the same sector look strong, diversification matters. Use the sector color bars to quickly check your exposure before adding another position in the same sector.
</Callout>
